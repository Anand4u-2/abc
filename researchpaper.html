Global Warming Data: A Research Analysis

Abstract

This paper investigates the trends and implications of global warming by analyzing historical temperature, CO₂ levels, and sea level data from NASA, NOAA, and IPCC. It identifies strong correlations between greenhouse gas concentrations and global temperature rise, calling for urgent action.

1. Introduction

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average temperature due to rising levels of greenhouse gases. This research utilizes scientific datasets to study the magnitude and rate of these changes.

2. Objectives

  • Analyze global temperature data (1880–2023)
  • Correlate CO₂ levels with temperature rise
  • Review sea level and ice melt trends

3. Data Sources and Methodology

Data is sourced from NASA GISS, NOAA, and IPCC. Analysis tools include Python and Excel. Temperature anomalies, CO₂ levels, and sea level rise are visualized and compared for trend evaluation.

4. Data Analysis

  • Temperature Rise: +1.1°C since 1880
  • CO₂ Levels: 420+ ppm in 2023
  • Sea Levels: ~9 inches rise since 1880
  • Ice Sheets: Arctic sea ice declining 13%/decade

5. Findings

There is a strong positive correlation (r ≈ 0.9) between CO₂ and temperature rise. Human activity is the dominant cause. Impacts include extreme weather, sea-level rise, and ecological disruption.

6. Mitigation and Adaptation

Mitigation: Renewable energy, reforestation, carbon reduction policies.

Adaptation: Infrastructure upgrades, sustainable practices, disaster response systems.

7. Conclusion

Empirical evidence confirms the accelerating pace of global warming. Immediate collective action is necessary to prevent irreversible damage to the planet and humanity.

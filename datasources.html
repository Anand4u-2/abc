Global Warming Data Sources

Here are the key data sources for global warming research. Each source provides valuable insights into the environmental changes affecting our planet.

1. NASA GISS (Goddard Institute for Space Studies)

NASA GISS provides temperature anomaly data, which is used globally to track climate change and warming trends.

  • NASA GISS Data

2. NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration)

NOAA offers extensive climate datasets, including historical climatology, greenhouse gas concentrations, and extreme weather data.

  • NOAA Climate Data

3. IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change)

The IPCC provides detailed reports synthesizing global climate change research, supporting policies on mitigating and adapting to climate change.

  • IPCC Reports

4. Mauna Loa Observatory (Scripps/NOAA CO₂ Data)

Mauna Loa is one of the longest-running and most accurate sources for atmospheric CO₂ data, illustrating the critical role of greenhouse gases in global warming.

  • Mauna Loa CO₂ Data

5. Our World in Data (OWID)

OWID provides visually rich, interactive charts and data on emissions, temperature trends, and climate policy, making complex datasets more accessible.

  • Our World in Data on CO₂

6. Berkeley Earth

Berkeley Earth offers independent temperature reconstructions and thorough analysis, ensuring data transparency and broad application in climate studies.

  • Berkeley Earth Data

7. European Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S)

The Copernicus Climate Change Service provides essential Earth observation data from satellites, which is critical for monitoring sea level rise, ice sheet loss, and more.

  • Copernicus Climate Data
